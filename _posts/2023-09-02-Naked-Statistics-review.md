I do some work with statistics, more and more in fact, and I'm always looking for good
books on the topic, both for my own edification, and also to recommend to people 
who want to know more about what is becoming (with the recent advances in ML and AI)
an important thing to have some grasp of in our field. 

Here's a good one that I recommend.

## Naked Statistics: Taking the Dread Out of the Data, Charles Wheelan, W.W. Norton and Company, 2013

It's written in a light style, with a lot of examples that are easy to follow (especially if you have some 
grasp of US sports). The mathematics are generally avoided, but then explained briefly in chapter end notes 
or appendixes. 

It covers:

* What's the point of statistics?
* Descriptive Statistics
* Deception using Descriptive Statistics
* Correlation
* Basic Probability
* (The Monty Hall Problem)
* Problems with Probability
* The Importance of Data
* The Central Limit Theorem
* Inference
* * Polling
* Regression Analysis
* Common Regression Mistakes
* Program Evaluation
* (an appendix on statistical software, which does mention R, but doesn't mention python libraries) 

It doesn't go into ML or AI, but as Regression Analysis is fundamental for much of that, it's a good place to start.

If you're looking for an accessible text to start learning about statistics, I'd recommend this one; it's only
about 300 pages, and will give you a good basis on the vocabulary of stats and some of the techniques used
to arm yourself to take on more advanced texts if you want to get deeper into this topic.
